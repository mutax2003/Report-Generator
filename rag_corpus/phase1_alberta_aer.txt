Alberta Phase I ESA — Ecoventure reference tone (Signum Consulting structure)
---

Executive summary structure (Signum Consulting Ltd. sample reports, Ecoventure voice):

1. Opening: "{Consultant} was contracted by {Client} to conduct a Phase I Environmental Site Assessment on the wellsite and associated facilities known as {Well name}."
2. Well history: spud date, cased date, re-entry and total depth, current status, production fluid.
3. Drilling waste: "The drilling waste was assessed using Checklist Compliance Option 1 (AER, 2014);" then volumes and disposal methods (LWD, landspray, landspread, remote haul).
4. Phase II for drilling waste if applicable; historical air photo observations; site visit status.
5. Closing: "After reviewing regulatory information and records, and conducting informational interviews; it is recommended that well centre and the production areas be investigated. The {Client} keyword is Phase II."

Ecoventure Inc. prepares Phase I Environmental Site Assessments for upstream oil and gas facilities in Alberta in support of AER Schedule Two and reclamation certificate applications. The assessment is non-intrusive and does not include subsurface sampling unless otherwise stated.

The scope includes review of regulatory records (AER, Alberta Environment and Protected Areas databases where applicable), historical land use, available aerial photography, and interviews with knowledgeable persons. Drilling waste management is documented against AER (2014) compliance options. Production infrastructure such as access roads, wellheads, berms, and storage tanks is described from file information and site reconnaissance when completed.

Where records or imagery indicate potential contamination or stressed vegetation near the well centre or production footprint, the report may recommend a Phase II ESA for delineation. Limitations include access constraints, completeness of third-party databases, and conditions at the time of the site visit only.

Qualified persons signing on behalf of Ecoventure Inc. must review all draft text before issuance to the client.
